The Critical Role of 40ML Reservoirs in African Water Security.

Executing zero-leakage bulk water storage in hostile topographies requires absolute mastery of post-tensioned concrete and mass earthworks.

As municipal grids across the Sub-Saharan region face unprecedented strain due to rapid urbanization and shifting climate patterns, the demand for high-capacity, highly resilient bulk water storage has never been greater.

For decades, Unicon Construction has been at the forefront of liquid-retaining infrastructure. A mega-reservoir—often exceeding 40 Megalitres (40,000,000 liters)—is not simply a concrete tank. It is a highly stressed, dynamic structural vessel that must withstand immense hydrostatic pressure without a single millimeter of variance.

The Post-Tensioning Advantage

Traditional reinforced concrete struggles to contain massive volumes of water without micro-cracking under outward pressure. To mitigate this, Unicon deploys advanced post-tensioning techniques in our circular reservoirs.

  • Active Compression: High-tensile steel cables are sleeved within the concrete walls. Once the concrete cures, these cables are hydraulically tensioned, actively squeezing the concrete together to counteract the outward force of the water.
  • Zero-Leak Tolerances: This active compression ensures that the concrete remains entirely crack-free, guaranteeing zero-leakage over a multi-decade operational lifespan.
  • Material Efficiency: Post-tensioning allows for thinner wall profiles, significantly reducing the amount of raw concrete required, thereby accelerating the construction schedule and reducing the carbon footprint of the build.

Mastering the Unsupported Dome

A hallmark of a modern Unicon reservoir is the unsupported concrete dome roof. In traditional construction, large roofs require internal supporting columns. However, internal columns in a water reservoir create weak points in the floor slab and complicate the waterproofing process.

By engineering a perfect, free-spanning curved dome, we eliminate the need for internal columns. This is achieved through highly complex, custom-fabricated steel shuttering and a continuous, synchronized concrete pour that requires our teams to work seamlessly, often late into the night, to prevent cold joints.

The result is a monolithic, hyper-resilient structure that protects the municipal or agricultural water supply from contamination, evaporation, and structural fatigue.
