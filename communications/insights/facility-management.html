Launching 2027: Predictive Facility Management.

Heavy infrastructure degrades rapidly in harsh African environments. How Unicon is shifting from reactive repair to continuous asset protection.

A building’s lifecycle does not end at practical completion. In fact, for commercial developers and public institutions, practical completion is merely day one of a multi-decade operational journey.

Across the African continent, harsh UV indexes, severe storm cycles, and corrosive coastal conditions cause heavy infrastructure to degrade rapidly. Historically, the industry has relied on a "break-fix" model—waiting for a catastrophic leak or mechanical failure before deploying repair teams.

Recognizing the massive capital loss this causes our clients, Unicon Construction is officially announcing the launch of our dedicated Tech-Enabled Facility Management Division, slated for rollout in early 2027.

Moving Beyond Reactive Repairs

The 2027 Facility Management rollout transitions Unicon from an infrastructure builder to a complete lifecycle partner. We are deploying advanced technology to monitor, maintain, and upgrade massive retail, agricultural, and industrial assets.

  • IoT Predictive Maintenance: By integrating continuous sensor monitoring into HVAC, refrigeration, and pump station networks, we track system health in real-time. This allows us to schedule proactive servicing before critical downtime affects operations.
  • Digital Twin Structural Audits: Utilizing drone photogrammetry and Lidar scanning, we create living "Digital Twins" of our clients' facilities. This enables hyper-accurate structural degradation reports, tracking concrete wear or roof integrity over time.
  • Sustainable Retrofits: Our FM teams will manage ongoing structural retrofits, waterproofing rehabilitation, and the integration of advanced energy-efficient systems to keep aging assets competitive.

Total Lifecycle Accountability

By operating across the Design, Build, and Manage phases, Unicon creates a closed loop of accountability. Our design teams learn from the maintenance data, ensuring our future builds are engineered to be even more resilient.

For our clients, this means a single point of contact. The same engineering minds that poured your foundations and erected your structural steel will be the ones actively monitoring and protecting your investment for decades to come.
